Jellof Rice Recipie

Description

A popular and flavorful West African dish known for its vibrant colors and rich taste. It is a one-pot rice dish that is often considered a staple in many West African countries,

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes or tomato puree
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: diced chicken, fish, or other protein of your choice

Directions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pot, and sauté for an additional minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, chopped red and green bell peppers, thyme, curry powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook the mixture for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the rice to the pot and stir to coat the rice with the tomato mixture.
  6. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed the liquid.
  7. Optional: If using diced chicken, fish, or other protein, add it to the pot about halfway through the cooking process and cook until fully cooked.
  8. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork and let it sit for a few minutes before serving.

Jollof rice is often served with side dishes like fried plantains, grilled chicken, or a side salad. Adjust the seasonings to your taste and enjoy this delicious West African dish!
